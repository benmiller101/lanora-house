📦 Step 1: Sorting Paper & Cardboard into Categories

All paper and cardboard are not the same — and recycling them efficiently starts with proper sorting. Here’s how materials are typically categorized:
	•	Corrugated Cardboard (used in boxes and packaging)
	•	Mixed Paper (junk mail, books, magazines)
	•	Office Paper (white sheets, printer paper)
	•	Newspapers & Newsprint
	•	Shredded Confidential Paper

When we clear a property, our team sorts these at source wherever possible. Mixed materials are separated from food-contaminated cardboard (which usually cannot be recycled) and grouped by type to maintain high recycling value.

⸻

🧊 Step 2: Keeping Paper & Cardboard Dry (Very Important!)

Moisture is the enemy of paper recycling.

That’s why at Lanora, all recyclable paper and cardboard are:
	•	Stored in dry, covered units
	•	Bailed if necessary to prepare for transport
	•	Kept separate from general or wet waste

This preserves the integrity of the fibres, making sure they can be fully recycled without being rejected by recycling facilities.

⸻

🏭 Step 3: Off to Local Recycling Facilities

Once sorted and stored, your old papers and boxes head off to licensed recycling partners in Cornwall and the South West.

Here’s what happens next:
	1.	Shredding – the paper is shredded into small pieces
	2.	Pulping – mixed with water and turned into a slurry
	3.	Screening & De-inking – contaminants like glue, plastic and ink are removed
	4.	Rolling into Sheets – clean pulp is pressed, dried and turned into new paper or cardboard

In fact, 70% of the paper used in the UK is recycled, and it takes 70% less energy to make recycled paper than to create it from raw materials【source: WRAP UK】.
