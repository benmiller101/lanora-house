🧼 Safe & Certified Disposal

Dead animals are classified as controlled waste and must be disposed of using licensed methods to avoid risks of:

Disease transmission (Hepatitis, Leptospirosis, etc.)

Airborne contamination

Infestation by flies, maggots, or vermin

Breaches of the Animal By-Products Regulations (ABPR)

Our team is fully trained in biohazardous waste removal and works with PPE, disinfectants, and industry-standard equipment to ensure your property is safe and odour-free.

❌ Can You Remove It Yourself?

While it's legal in some cases to dispose of small dead animals yourself (e.g. rodents), there are risks and legal limitations:

Dead foxes, badgers or deer should never go in household bins

It's illegal to bury or burn fallen livestock

Private property = your responsibility, not the council’s

Waste must be removed by a licensed carrier

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

Can I put a dead animal in my bin?
For small animals like mice or rats, yes — but it’s not advised due to hygiene risks. For anything larger (e.g. fox, cat, badger), it must be handled by a licensed waste carrier like us.

Will the council remove a dead animal from private land?
No – it’s your responsibility. Councils only remove animals from public roads and spaces.

Do you clean and disinfect the area after removal?
Yes – we provide a full sanitisation service, including deodorisation, to ensure the site is safe and hygienic.

Is this service discreet?
Absolutely – our vehicles are unbranded and our team is trained to work respectfully and without drawing attention.
